About

Blending the timeless elegance of jazz with the versatility of multiple genres, Five Note Theory is a dynamic vocal ensemble that seamlessly weaves together intricate harmonies and expressive storytelling. With roots in jazz but a repertoire that spans classical, choral, pop, soul, hip-hop, and beyond, this five-member group brings a fresh and sophisticated sound to every performance.


The ensemble came together through a shared passion for vocal artistry, with its members drawing from diverse musical backgrounds. Cindy, a renowned music educator and choral director, is shaping the next generation of singers as an educator at Miami Arts Studio, and Jormilit, a former student of the same institution, is a vocal jazz student at Western Michigan University. Asriel, Leonard, and Stephen expounded their musical foundation at Oakwood University, where they immersed themselves in a tradition of vocal excellence. Though their paths varied, all five members share the unique bond of having sung with the Jason Max Ferdinand Singers, an experience that refined their precision, blend, and deep musicality.


With a sound shaped by legendary groups like Take 6, The Manhattan Transfer, The Hi-Lo’s, The Four Freshmen, and New York Voices, their repertoire reflects a love for rich harmonies and expressive storytelling. From classic jazz standards and bossa nova grooves to reimagined rock and pop oldies, their setlist is as diverse as their influences. They also bring a soulful depth to sacred choral works, crafting powerful interpretations of sacred texts that resonate with both spiritual and secular audiences alike.


Whether delivering a silky-smooth jazz ballad, a rhythmically charged pop arrangement, or an ethereal choral setting, Five Note Theory is committed to pushing vocal boundaries while staying true to the art of close harmony singing. With a sound that is both nostalgic and forward-thinking, they invite listeners on a journey through the rich landscape of vocal music—one note at a time.
